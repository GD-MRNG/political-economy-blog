## THE GROUND PICTURE

The material reality for working professionals in Singapore is currently defined by a sharp contradiction. While the **Straits Times Index** has shown resilience and real wages grew by 3.2% in the last year, many young professionals report feeling financially inadequate. This sense of "middle-class precarity" is driven by a cost stack that remains heavy despite high nominal salaries.

At the same time, the job market is undergoing a structural transformation. Large-scale layoffs at firms like **Meta**, **Standard Chartered**, and **Intuit** are not merely cyclical downturns but signals of a shift toward an AI-driven economy. Work that was once considered stable is being redesigned or moved to lower-cost regional hubs, creating a climate of professional anxiety.

## ECONOMY AND COST OF LIVING

The cost of living remains a primary pressure point as global volatility lands directly on the household budget. **Grab** has extended its fuel surcharge and higher metered fare rates until the end of July due to fuel price volatility. Electricity bills are also under pressure as the energy crisis persists, and households are being advised on managing "vampire energy" usage to mitigate rising tariffs.

Housing conditions show a widening gap between different segments of the market. While the government is likely to exceed its target of 55,000 **Build-To-Order (BTO)** flats by 2027, private residential vacancy has risen to 6.2%. However, luxury non-landed home rents rose for the sixth straight quarter, and **Executive Condominium (EC)** units remain expensive, prompting new government rules to prioritize first-time buyers.

Food prices are being impacted by both regional supply shifts and global conflict. The conflict in the Middle East poses risks to Singapore’s import-dependent food security, while regional neighbors like Indonesia and Malaysia are shifting crops toward fuel production. Locally, even niche trends like the "Dubai chocolate" craze are seeing price hikes due to rising ingredient costs like pistachios.

The **Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)** is maintaining a stance of heightened vigilance. The core dynamic is a persistent rise in essential costs—transport, energy, and food—that is outpacing the perceived benefits of wage growth for many professionals.

## EMPLOYMENT AND INDUSTRY

The employment landscape is being reshaped by the rapid adoption of **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**. The government has signaled that Singapore must not have "jobless growth" during this transition, yet retrenchments are occurring in both tech and traditional manufacturing. **Gardenia** is moving its bakery production to Malaysia, resulting in 141 local job losses, following similar moves by firms like **Yeo’s** and **Tiger Beer**.

In the financial sector, the shift is equally pronounced. **Standard Chartered** is planning to cut nearly 8,000 jobs as part of its AI advancement strategy, while **DBS** is hiring 500 young talents specifically for AI roles. This suggests a "replacement" cycle where entry-level work is being fundamentally altered, requiring fresh graduates to have significant internship experience and soft skills to remain competitive.

The professional services sector is also under pressure to redesign roles. The **Skills Framework for Accountancy** has been updated to include AI and sustainability capabilities, reflecting a broader trend where 38 job roles in accounting are expected to undergo significant change. Burnout remains a critical issue, with reports indicating that one in three new lawyers wants to leave the industry.

The core dynamic is a transition where traditional roles are being automated or outsourced, while new roles require a high degree of technical specialization.

## GOVERNMENT DIRECTION

The government is directing significant capital and regulatory energy into two main pillars: **AI Integration** and **Population Ageing**. Under the **National AI Strategy 2.0**, the government has partnered with **Google** and **OpenAI**, with the latter committing $300 million to enhance local AI skills. Sector-wide AI projects have commenced, beginning with the aviation industry to optimize airport operations.

To address the challenges of a "super-aged" society, the government launched a $350 million **Longevity Challenge** research program. This initiative targets cognitive decline and physical function loss among seniors. Additionally, **MediSave** withdrawal limits for HIV drugs have been increased from $550 to $850 monthly, reflecting a broader push to manage long-term healthcare costs through policy adjustments.

Regulatory focus is also tightening around digital safety and corporate transparency. New laws now require government approval for major media acquisitions, and the **SGX RegCo** has mandated that suspended firms must rectify issues within three years or face delisting. The government is also moving away from using **PES** status for National Servicemen, signaling a modernization of administrative classifications.

The core direction is the aggressive subsidization of AI adoption and healthcare infrastructure to future-proof the economy against demographic decline.

## REGIONAL POSITION

Singapore’s regional position is being tested by the rising industrial ambitions of its neighbors. The **Johor-Singapore Special Economic Zone (JS-SEZ)** is nearing completion, which may offer a release valve for costs but also increases the pull of manufacturing and logistics jobs toward Malaysia. Johor is actively looking to Singapore for inspiration on digital vouchers and urban redevelopment, while also promoting a distinct "Bangsa Johor" state identity.

Indonesia is making moves to gatekeep its major commodity exports, aiming to compete with global trading giants. This shift in trade policy, along with **Prabowo’s** export control moves, could increase state intervention in regional markets and affect Singapore’s role as a trading hub. Meanwhile, **Vietnam** is positioning itself as a global semiconductor hub with US support, creating new competition for high-tech investment.

Infrastructure developments like China’s **Pinglu Canal** are expected to boost trade links between ASEAN and China’s heartlands. However, regional security remains a concern, with **Japan** and the **Philippines** initiating talks on an intelligence-sharing pact. Singapore continues to emphasize that it must engage China on its own terms while maintaining stable ties with the US.

The core dynamic is a shift toward deeper integration with Johor while facing increased competition from Indonesia and Vietnam for industrial dominance.

## GLOBAL FORCES LANDING LOCALLY

The ongoing conflict in the Middle East and the crisis in the **Strait of Hormuz** are the most significant global forces landing in Singapore. This has led to "war-risk" underwriting tightening for airlines and shipping, forcing rerouting and increasing the cost of imported goods. Singapore is actively monitoring fuel emergencies for flights and has guaranteed sufficient **Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG)** supplies only until the end of 2026.

The **US-China** rivalry continues to create a fractured world order that complicates Singapore’s neutral stance. While the **Trump-Xi** summit provided a temporary "positive tone," the US is weighing new chip tariffs to spur its own domestic growth. This creates supply chain uncertainty for Singapore’s semiconductor and IT industries, as seen in the recent crackdown on the smuggling of **Nvidia** chips through regional channels.

## WHAT TO WATCH

The launch of the **Johor-Singapore Special Economic Zone** in early 2027 will determine the new flow of labor and capital across the Causeway.

The impact of the **Strait of Hormuz** crisis on long-term electricity tariffs will become clearer as current LNG hedges expire.

The effectiveness of the **SkillsFuture** tracking system will be tested as the first wave of AI-displaced workers attempts to re-enter the workforce.

The outcome of the **US Department of Justice** investigation into price-fixing allegations against local shipping veterans may affect Singapore’s maritime reputation.

The implementation of the **Veterinary Council** and other new professional regulatory bodies will signal the start of stricter standards across service sectors.
