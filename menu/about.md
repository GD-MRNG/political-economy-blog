This site publishes analysis of political economy at three scales: the global system, the technology sector, and the lived experience of Singapore and Southeast Asia.

Each report synthesizes key global developments and delivers disciplined analysis across geopolitics, economics, security, and emerging risks. Rather than chasing headlines, the focus is on identifying meaningful shifts, connecting strategic signals, and assessing their broader implications for the international system.

The mission is simple: to document, track, and evaluate consequential global change through consistent, methodical weekly reporting.

## Why This Exists

This blog is part of my study of political economy — and a response to a problem I kept running into.

The world is changing quickly. The structures that governed trade, security, energy, and technology for the past several decades are visibly fracturing. Most people can feel it, but few have been given the tools to make sense of it. That is not a personal failing. It is a gap in how we are taught to understand the world. Most of what passes for public discourse offers ideology or commentary — frameworks that guide you toward certain conclusions without giving you the means to evaluate them independently.

Without structural thinking, you navigate by what others tell you to believe. You can follow events, but you cannot read them. You cannot see the forces underneath, trace the connections, or anticipate where things are heading. It is a bit like living without depth perception — you can see what is in front of you, but you cannot judge distance or direction.

I started putting this work out for two reasons: to sharpen my own understanding, and to offer others a way to widen their perspective. Not to provide answers, but to build the habit of asking better questions — and to make the connections that turn a news cycle into a legible picture of how the world actually works.
